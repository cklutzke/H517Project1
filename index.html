Cholera Outbreak: London, 1854


In 1854, an outbreak of cholera in the Soho district of London killed 616 people. Physician John Snow theorized that the disease was related to contaminated water. He created a map that showed where the deaths occurred in relation to water pumps in the area (the black diamonds), and showed they were strongly correlated with a water pump on Broad Street.
